Summary of the Stream

This stream investigates the correlation between peer relationships and academic engagement among grade 10 students at APEC Schools. The study examines various aspects of peer interactions, including positive ones like collaborative learning and negative impacts such as peer pressure and bullying. Our stream provides insights into fostering positive peer relationships, benefiting both students and adults in various settings by offering strategies for building and maintaining healthy connections. And we also learned about effective marketing strategies; how you communicate with the students, and also promoting our live stream.

Stream Advantages

Viewer engagement - We believe that this is one of our group’s strengths as our group can communicate not only through our members but also our viewers making them feel more comfortable and welcomed to our stream. The more viewers feel comfortable with us, the more comfortable the environment of the stream becomes which can cause positive growths. Not only could our stream give opportunity for our viewers to learn and grow along with us with our topics, they can also help us to learn and grow through questions.

Adaptation - This is one of our group’s strengths as our group was met with several obstacles. So we were able to formulate alternatives while still maintaining a positive attitude for the stream. Keeping the viewers engaged rather than them leaving our stream.

Areas of Improvement

Content collabs and a consistent streaming schedule allows for audience engagement and the potential for new viewers. Engaging with an audience and building a community creates that more personal connection that makes people want to come back for more. The more one streams, the more opportunity there is to view content, and thus more sets of eyes can be on the channel. Professionalism is created by professional equipment; clean sound and visuals create digestible, refined experiences. Recognition and entertainment comes from a visible branding experience compounded by collaborations.

Marketing and Engagement

Around a week before the planned stream date, we created a poster and placed it outside the door of 10-2. This was our physical poster for advertising our stream and we focused more of our marketing efforts on social media. One day before the stream several of our group members posted our poster on social media, advertising it to friends and family. Finally, on the day of the stream, we sent an email announcing to all grade levels in V. Luna that our live stream was up and running. During our live stream, we had some decent viewer engagement. According to our Twitch analytics, we had 3 chat message spikes of activity throughout the entire stream. We also gained 7 new followers over the whole stream, which means that some viewers are ready to return to our stream whenever we stream again. Overall, we had decent success with marketing and engagement, as we had most of our viewers and followers come from our direct advertising according to the statistics.

Performance Analysis

Over the course of our entire stream, there was a consistent average of around 10 viewers. It’s somewhere in the middle where I consider to be high or low for viewership. I consider single digits like 5 viewers to be “low viewership”, whereas I consider double digits like 15+ viewers to be “high viewership”. And we had a total of 59 unique viewers that interacted with our livestream. According to our Twitch channel’s overview, we had a peak viewership of 19 viewers, which roughly stayed consistent during the whole time. Unfortunately due to connection problems that popped up in the middle of streaming, there was a slight decrease in live viewers according to our graph. Interestingly, we had a spike in average viewers right before we lost connection, and had another spike in average viewers right after our stream came back online. I believe that as a whole, we had pretty decent viewership count, considering we had a lackluster promotion strategy that resulted in viewers having little time to prepare time to watch our premiere livestream.
